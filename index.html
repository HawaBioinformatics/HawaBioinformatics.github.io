About Me

I am a biology-trained educator with a background in bioinformatics, passionate about making complex scientific concepts accessible to younger learners. Before entering the education field, I studied and worked in bioinformatics, where I explored how data and computational tools can be used to understand living systems. Through this project, I aim to bridge the gap between advanced scientific fields and early STEM education by creating kid-friendly lessons, resources, and activities. My goal is to introduce topics like bioinformatics, data-driven science, and computational thinking to middle and high school students in relatable and engaging ways. I believe students don not need to wait until high school or college to explore specialized sciences. By connecting real-world applications to everyday classroom topics, I hope to spark curiosity and inspire the next generation of scientists, problem-solvers, and innovators.
